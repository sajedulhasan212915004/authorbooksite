Biography

Dr. Abdulqadir Abukar Mursal's journey into psychology began with a deep curiosity about human behavior and mental processes. Born and raised in Somalia, he experienced firsthand the challenges faced by communities in accessing quality mental health education and services. This early exposure shaped his commitment to making psychological knowledge accessible to Somali-speaking populations.

His academic journey took him through rigorous training in psychology, where he distinguished himself through his dedication to both theoretical understanding and practical application. Dr. Mursal's research interests span developmental psychology, social psychology, and the cultural factors that influence mental health and well-being.

At Mogadishu University, Dr. Mursal has established himself as a beloved educator and mentor. His teaching philosophy emphasizes critical thinking, cultural sensitivity, and the application of psychological principles to real-world challenges. Students consistently praise his ability to make complex concepts understandable and relevant to their lives and communities.

Beyond the classroom, Dr. Mursal is actively involved in community outreach programs, professional development workshops, and public education initiatives. His work has contributed significantly to reducing stigma around mental health issues and promoting psychological well-being across Somali communities.
